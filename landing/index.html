Questions developers ask

Everything you need to know before your legal team asks.

What happens if I don't comply with Texas HB 18?

Texas can fine your company $10,000 per violation and force removal from app stores. The law applies to any app with 50%+ social features accessed by Texas users. Don't risk it — compliance takes 10 minutes with Copply.

Do you store personal information?

No. We hash user IDs with SHA-256 and store only the hash. Declared ages are stored as numbers, not tied to identity. Parent emails in consent records are encrypted at rest. We designed for privacy-first compliance.

How does parental consent work?

Three methods: (1) Email verification link sent to parent, (2) Small $0.30 credit card charge (immediately refunded) to verify adult status, (3) Government ID photo upload. The method is auto-selected based on state strictness.

What if a state law changes?

We monitor all pending legislation. When laws change, we update the SDK and edge functions automatically. Your app gets the new rules without a code change — just the compliance config updates in our dashboard.

Can I self-host?

Enterprise plans include on-premise deployment. The entire stack is open-source: Supabase PostgreSQL + Edge Functions + Next.js dashboard. You can run it in your own infrastructure with a support contract.

What platforms does the SDK support?

React Native, Expo, and Flutter today. Pure JavaScript/TypeScript SDK for web apps coming next. iOS and Android native wrappers are on the Enterprise roadmap. The API is REST-based so any platform can call it directly.
